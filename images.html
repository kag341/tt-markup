Scalable Vector Graphics (.svg)

Embed .svg into an image tag

Use alt text


<img src="star.svg" alt="star" width="106" height="106"/>

Use aria-label


<img src="star.svg" aria-label="star" width="106" height="106"/>

Use aria-labelled by

Star


<p id="startext">Star</p>
<img src="star.svg" aria-labelledby="startext" width="106" height="106"/>

Use inline svg (use role="img")

Use title text - Mostly Supported


<svg width="120" height="120" viewBox="200 40 400 400" role="img">
<title>Star</title>

Benefit of using an .svg over a .png, .jpg, or .gif

If an .svg is resized, the image does not lose crispness, unline other image types. For example, the two images below are an .svg on the left and a .png on the right. If your browser setting is at 100%, they look the same. If you use your browser settings to increase the size of the screen to 175%. The image on the left retains its shape, while the .png on the right becomes fuzzy. This could become an issue for users with low-vision.
